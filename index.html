01 The Lighthouse
08:00AM - 10:00PM
The lighthouse in Raffles Marina opens daily from 8 a.m. - 10 p.m. Visitors can just walk-in for free!
Located at the tip of the Club's breakwater is Raffles Marina's own lighthouse. Built in 1994 and overlooking the Tuas Second Link – Singapore's second causeway to Malaysia, this 12 metres high icon flashes every 10 seconds with a beam that reaches 15 metres. The lighthouse is maintained by the Club and is the only lighthouse not owned and operated by the Maritime Port Authority of Singapore. The beacon is a popular spot for photography groups.During the evenings, soak in the expanse of the beautiful sunset as you take a stroll down the pier to the lighthouse after a dinner

03 SEA PORT
Raffles Marina is a gazetted CIQP Marina, authorised to handle the clearing in or out of visiting foreign yachts and cruising resident yachts, in Singapore.


Boating Course

The course is required for operators of engine-powered or jet-propelled pleasure craft (with SZ or SZH license). It ensures that boaters are equipped with the necessary skills and knowledge to navigate their craft safely within the port waters. The course also allows relevant enforcement agencies to easily identify the operator.

Signature Cruise

Raffles Marina has made a name for itself, organising short, medium and long range cruises. Our flotillas to Phuket, Anambas and the Riau Archipelago are legendary. With cruising previously confined to the east coast of Malaysia, Raffles Marina has been credited with opening up the north-western and southern theatres for cruising.
